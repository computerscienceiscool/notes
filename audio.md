# 🎶 Music brief for the linuxGirl channel

Hi! 💜 Your music would be the soundtrack for **linuxGirl** — a TikTok channel that teaches Linux to total beginners. The whole vibe is *calm, friendly, and "this isn't scary."* Videos are vertical and short (about **20–40 seconds**). You'd be credited as the musician (in the captions and the channel bio).

No pressure on style — these are just starting ideas. Make it yours!
---
## What would help most (roughly in order)

### 1. A signature "sting" — 3 to 5 seconds
A tiny, memorable little sound that plays at the **start (or end) of every video** — like a mini theme song. Over time it becomes *the* linuxGirl sound that people recognize. 
- **Vibe:** friendly, warm, a little sparkly / curious / "techy" — **not** dark  or hacker-movie scary.
- **Length:** 3–5 seconds.

### 2. Background loops — a couple of moods
Quiet instrumental beds that play softly *under* the video. Two would cover almost everything we make:
- **Calm & reassuring** — for the gentle, beginner-comfort videos.
- **Light & upbeat** — for the faster "here's a cool trick" videos.
- *(Optional bonus: a happy little 2–3 second "ta-da" for the end of a video.)*

Please make each loop **loop cleanly** (the end flows smoothly back into the start) so it can be stretched to any length. **~15–30 seconds each is plenty.**
---

## A few simple tech notes
- **Instrumental** please (no vocals) — so a voiceover can sit on top later.
- **WAV is ideal** (44.1 kHz). A high-quality MP3 or M4A (320 kbps) is fine too.
- Try to keep the **volume roughly even** across the tracks.
- **One file per track**, named simply — e.g. `sting.wav`, `calm-loop.wav`,   `upbeat-loop.wav`.
---

## Quick copyright check (just so we're safe on TikTok) 🛡️
TikTok scans audio, so we want everything to be 100% yours:
- ✅ Anything made in **FL Studio with its own built-in instruments and sounds**  is perfect and totally safe to use.
- ⚠️ The one thing to avoid: **loops or sample packs downloaded from elsewhere**   (Splice, other producers' packs, sounds pulled from YouTube, etc.) — those can  trigger copyright flags on TikTok *even if the rest of the song is yours*.
- 🤔 If you're ever not sure whether a sound counts as "yours" vs. a downloaded  pack, just flag it and we'll check it together — no worries either way.
- 🌟 Best of all: **brand-new tracks you haven't posted anywhere yet** (not on   your YouTube, etc.), so TikTok can't mistake them for someone else's.
---
